* ⚙️ **Automates QA/QC** across mechanical, electrical, structural and more

Launch Video: https://youtu.be/CRFsqOTynng

### **❌ The Problem**

The construction industry's reliance on manual "spot-checking" for design errors costs **$177 billion** annually to fix mistakes that are discovered too late.

Senior Engineers spend up to **50%** of their time cross-referencing 100-page drawing sets against 2,000-page building regulations by hand. This process is slow, tedious, and unreliable.

Errors that are missed become exponentially more expensive to correct once construction is underway, causing massive project delays and budget overruns.

---

### **⚙️ The Solution**

Structured AI is the **AI Quality Assurance platform.**

1. **Proprietary Vision & Agents:** We trained a foundational vision model on **thousands** of engineering symbols to read plans like an expert.
2. **100x Faster, 150% More Errors:** Our AI checks 100-page blueprints against 2,000 pages of building codes **in minutes.**
3. **Cross-trade Checks:** Learns firm standards to detect inconsistencies across documents, drawings and Revit models, reducing cross-trade clashes before they occur.

### **🏗️ Why Construction Engineering?**

Brandon started coding and making games when he was 12. At 17, a YouTube video he made demonstrating a fighter jet trained with deep reinforcement learning to dodge missiles went viral. This led to him being scouted by a US construction contractor to apply that same algorithm to MEP pipe routing optimization. He then spent the next six years, throughout high school and university, contracting and building AI tools for the construction and engineering industry. He later led innovation and scaled AI tools in-house at a top US engineering firm, ultimately spinning out with his team to form Structured AI.

Raymond initially pursued architecture and engineering, but was talked out of the profession during an internship. Junior architects warned him about the extensive manual "grunt work" that defined their roles, a prospect that deterred him so much he switched his studies to Maths & Stats at Oxford. He now views his work freeing engineers from these very tasks as a full-circle moment, allowing them to focus on their passions.
