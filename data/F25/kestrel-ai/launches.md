# **The** **Problem**

Running Kubernetes at scale is **chaos**.\
\
As founding engineers on the Kubernetes Security team at Illumio, we saw both startups and large **organizations struggle with its complexity and scale**. For example, network policies are so tricky that many teams default to allowing everything, and even basic ops tasks like debugging failed rollouts mean spending hours digging through logs and events just to find where things went wrong.\
\
Over time, this complexity compounds and clusters begin to drift - configs diverge, policies break, and “temporary” workarounds become production. And when something goes wrong, **engineers are buried under alerts and reports** from disconnected tools, trying to debug blind at 3 a.m.

# **Our Solution**

Kestrel is redefining how teams manage Kubernetes with the **first agentic platform that unifies ops and security**. It replaces manual triage with autonomous investigations and human-in-the-loop remediations - so issues that used to take hours to debug can be understood and fixed in seconds.
