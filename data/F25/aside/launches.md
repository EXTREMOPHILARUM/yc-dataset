## ❌ Problem: The Knowledge Gap in Sales

Sales reps often freeze when faced with technical questions. Like:

> Are HSTS or strict transport policies enforced during data transmission?

Most say, _“Let me get back to you”,_ and the deal cools off. That’s why teams burn $60K+ a year on coaching, mock calls, and LMS exams. I’ve paid that bill too.

But onboarding drags. Products change fast. Tech-savvy AEs are rare.

What if your reps could pull the right answers instantly– straight from your best calls, API docs, or Slack threads?

## ✨ Solution: Let your reps cheat on sales calls

[Aside](https://asidehq.com/?utm_source=launch_yc) listens to your sales calls and surfaces real-time answers pulled from your docs, HubSpot, or past calls. It's like pressing **𝚌𝚝𝚛𝚕+𝚟** on your company’s knowledge into every rep – so anyone can sound like your best rep.

# How it works

\*\*🔎 Get answers from your docs, Slack, and past winning calls.\*\*\
When a tough question hits, just click **“How can I answer?”** - the right answer lands in 800ms. No guessing, no “let me get back to you”.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=95050&key=user_uploads/1576589/873df5e6-aeb0-4bc6-bc89-ff00031ecf4c)

📝 **Live coaching that catches what you miss**\
When you miss a key pain point, Aside flags it live — before the deal goes cold. It nudges what to ask next and coaches you using your playbook — BANT, SPICED, whatever your team runs on.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=95050&key=user_uploads/1576589/667d0f76-a05a-46ff-9d2f-4fa8a781f218)

💬 **Preload your cheatsheets as Live Cards**\
Turn your best answers into in-call cards that pop up right when you need them. No more flipping tabs — your best answers, ready the moment the question hits.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=95050&key=user_uploads/1576589/d9b7fd18-89bb-418d-a733-85713ab3f4a1)

**🎓 Coaching feedback, remembered by AI**\
After the call, Aside reviews what happened, gives you feedback, and turns it into live suggestions for the next one - so you never repeat the same mistake.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=95050&key=user_uploads/1576589/efb28b23-26ec-49b4-a50e-a66616439ca6)
