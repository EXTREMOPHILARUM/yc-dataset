# 🏭 The Problem

Manufacturing is an industry that has been all but left behind for the past 20 years compared to the advances in software development, self-driving vehicles, and all other fields that AI has revolutionised.

**Labour shortages**

Manufacturing of defence equipment, construction machines, and automotive make up the backbone of much of the American economy. In the U.S, there are 400,000 unfilled welding positions, and that is expected to rise to 600,000 by 2030.

**Push to reshore**

OEMs and Tier 1 manufacturers are pushing to reshore their manufacturing, but traditional methods of operation mean that they are failing to operate profitably when competing with manufacturing in offshore economies.

**Failure of current automation**

Current industrial robots can only achieve a very narrow set of well-defined manufacturing tasks because of the way robots are programmed. Traditionally programmed welding robots end up with around 10% of parts needing rework due to the delta in geometry from part to part.

# 🦾 Our solution

Forge provides a vision system and robotic intelligence that creates a sub-mm map, identifies all features and weld seams, and autonomously controls the welding robot arms.

Metal fabrication companies will now:

* No longer have to **manually program** robotic arms
* Achieve **99% first pass line yield**, up from the current 90%
* Use robotic welding arms in many previously infeasible situations
