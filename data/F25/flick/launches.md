### **Problem**

Many AI tools can give you 8-second casual clips to post on social media, but when it comes to making high-quality films, they don’t come close due to their lack of flexibility, creative control, and fragmented creative flow.

Creators think very differently from consumers. Creators think in a non-linear way — moving back and forth freely to iterate, making decisions at every step. To direct a film, creators need not just visual and character consistency, but also character movement, shot angles, camera language, and emotional arc.

### **Solution**

Flick puts creative power back in the hands of filmmakers:

1. **Infinite Canvas + Chat**

   An infinite canvas where users upload or generate text, images, and video as connected nodes. We integrate multiple models, but instead of prompt-to-video, we let filmmakers think in terms of scripts, characters, and scenes and give them a natural “chat to create on canvas” experience.

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=95208&key=user_uploads/2980671/b8812b5c-bb57-4071-9c06-8fe98632f7d0)

2. **Editing while creating**

   Creators like to go back and forth when making shots, so we built editing tools directly into the canvas. So, they can define exactly what kind of shot they want to add or delete to ensure a stronger narrative.

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=95208&key=user_uploads/2980671/03442527-7251-4885-9368-05ebffdb3191)

3. **Studio + Perplexity for film scenes**

   No one creates from nothing, we’ve needed inspiration since the beginning of humanity. That’s why we’re building [Studio](https://flick.art/studio/experiments-too-boring?from=home), where we produce our own [AI films](https://flick.art/studio/short-films-a-letter-in-early-fall?from=home) and collaborate with leading filmmakers to share their behind-the-scenes workflows as reusable canvases and templates for our community members.

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=95208&key=user_uploads/2980671/9c2e48c5-fdcc-4228-9782-250c0b8325d6)

   And integrated Perplexity for film search, allowing users to find classic film shots as references.
