---

## **The Problem**

Law firms are overpaying for basic answering services that do little more than take messages.

* **Missed after-hours calls:** Most law firms only accept intake during business hours, losing many clients that call after-hours.
* **Limited Intake Staff:** Not every incoming potential client is vetted and then duly followed up with, because intake staff is already exhausted with a lot of work, and can cost $42K a year per intake specialist.
* **High costs:** Services like Answering Legal charge $3.91/minute, and Ruby charges $235/month for just 50 minutes, they employ a human sweatshop somewhere in Georgia and sometimes in India which potential clients do not prefer.
* **Wasted leads:** Many law firms turn away the majority of inbound leads without monetizing them through referrals.

---

## **Our Solution – AI Call Intake with our Lead Referral Network**

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=87857&key=user_uploads/455465/5e7010ea-bb2a-4c8d-a7d0-8a825fbffac3)

Dench replaces expensive human receptionists with AI voice agents that:

* **Qualify leads in real-time:** Our NLP-driven AI assesses lead quality during calls, not after.
* **Offer 24/7 availability:** No missed calls, no lost leads.
* **Integrate seamlessly with CRMs:** Auto-syncs data to platforms like Clio Grow, eliminating manual entry.
* **Monetize unqualified leads:** Converts rejected cases into paid referrals through a compliant network. We transfer the call to another AI representing another law firm that handles that kind of cases, and micro-transact the lead live, helping law firm earn for the referred lead.
* **Schedule Consultations Automagically:** Intake staff would not have to call them back for anything as our AI handles all the followups, payments and everything right up to the end of the pipeline.

* **Detailed analytics:** Track call performance, lead quality, and conversion rates.

# **⚠️ The Problem**

**Creating comics is a labor-intensive process** that requires significant time and effort.

Despite the popularity of comics, producing them often **takes hours of meticulous work** using tools like Adobe Illustrator, or requires plain drawings. This limits the number of creators who can participate and the volume of content available to readers.

The comic reading experience has been the same for decades. On the other hand, **readers are looking for new and more immersive experiences.** While static images are the norm, there is a growing demand for interactive content that brings characters and stories to life with character voices and sounds.
