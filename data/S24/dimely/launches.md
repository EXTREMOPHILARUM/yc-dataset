## The Problem

3 billion people use Google Workspace each month and hundreds of millions use Google Sheets in their everyday work. Finance teams are increasingly moving work into Google Sheets because it comes free with Google Workspace, and it’s easier to aggregate data and collaborate in Sheets than in Excel.

The problem? Google Sheets lacks proper tooling for finance teams to streamline their work. As this move to Sheets picks up speed, finance teams are often forced to download files into Excel to complete the work, then re-upload them back into Drive to share once complete.

It also doesn’t help that Excel, over the last 40+ years, has built massive market share in finance with a community of finance educators and tooling, while Google Sheets has focused more on the data analyst and lacks many education touch points that would drive adoption by finance teams. It’s especially frustrating for finance teams, many of whom have Excel training, to lose access to the features and functionality they’re used to.

# **❌ Problem: Billing for B2B SaaS is manual and error-prone**

This is because:

* **Contracts are complex.** Contracts have multiple SKUs, payment schedules, pricing models, and billing metrics
* **Contracts are unstructured.** Terms are often bespoke and written in by sales teams trying to close deals
* **Data lives in different places.** Usage-based pricing models in particular, require matching contract data in your CRM with usage data from your data warehouse that is not always accurate or structured in a way that works well for billing 
* **Finance needs are deprioritized.** Engineering typically prioritizes product building and deprioritizes finance initiatives
* **High finance tech debt.** Billing systems that work early on remain in place for too long because it takes 5+ months to migrate to another system, resulting in a high number of manual workarounds, typically in spreadsheets
