**tl;dr**

Today, we’re excited to introduce Sarah, our 24/7 AI Admissions Coordinator.

Sarah qualifies new leads and books intakes automatically, helping clinics boost new patient volume by up to 30%. She manages 100% of inbound calls - handling follow-ups, reschedules, and medication refill requests - ensuring no message ever falls through the cracks.

With Sarah, clinics stay responsive and scalable - no extra hires, no added overhead.

**Our Journey**

We’re Dominik and Efren, on a mission to make mental healthcare accessible and affordable for everyone, everywhere. We met in 2018 while scaling a hyperscale startup - Efren as a founding engineer, and Dom as the first operator after starting his career at BCG. **The Problem**

Nearly 50 million Americans live with a mental health condition and that number grows by 1.5 million each year. Meanwhile, the U.S. is facing a growing shortage of 30,000 mental health clinicians, making it harder to meet rising demand.

But the real crisis isn’t just the clinician shortage - it’s that the antiquated, fragmented systems around them can’t keep up.

Even when providers are available, clinics struggle to manage the load. Intake calls go unanswered. Reschedules and refill requests fall through the cracks. Front desk staff are buried under hundreds of voicemails, forms, and follow-ups every week. Clinicians themselves spend more than half their time on documentation and admin, not with patients.

This operational drag is a massive bottleneck. Patients wait weeks for care, staff burn out, and clinics lose revenue on missed appointments and delayed treatment.

The result is a fragmented system that costs the U.S. healthcare system $300 billion annually due to delayed treatment, poor access, and administrative inefficiencies.

**The Solution**

One year ago, we launched our AI Clinical Assistant to help clinicians reclaim their time by automating clinical documentation, pre-charting, and treatment planning. It's now been used in over 1 million patient sessions.

Now we’ve launched Sarah, our AI Admissions Coordinator, to help patients access care faster by eliminating missed calls, delays, and administrative friction.

Sarah answers every call instantly, 24/7, no voicemails, no hold music. She qualifies new patients and books intakes, while also managing reschedules, cancellations, med refill requests, and appointment confirmations for existing patients.

Clinics using Sarah have seen up to a 30% increase in new patient bookings, while ensuring that 100% of existing patient inquiries are handled, without adding headcount.

**The Future We Are Building**

We believe the future of care will be driven by AI-first teams, where clinicians are surrounded by AI agents that handle everything outside the session, so they can focus on what matters most: seeing patients.

But we’re not just building the agents, we’re building the workspace where they collaborate, hand off tasks, and get things done.

# The Problem

Mental health issues are a growing concern in America, with approximately 50 million Americans currently grappling with a mental health condition. Alarmingly, this number is increasing by 1.5 million each year.

Concurrently, the US is on track to face a shortfall of 30,000 mental health clinicians by 2024, a situation that's already costing the US health system $300 billion annually due to delayed treatment.

In a world where time is of the essence, clinicians are spending only 50% of their day with patients, while the rest is consumed by tasks like clinical documentation, dealing with insurance, and other back-office heavy work.

# Our Solution

We power mental health clinicians with mdhub’s clinical AI assistant, enabling them to focus on patient care.

The patient-clinician conversation is at the core of mental health treatment, and by capturing it, our clinical AI assistant can automate tasks that prevent clinicians from dedicating more time to patients, such as writing clinical documentation, drafting patient after-visit reports, pre-charting, and treatment planning.
