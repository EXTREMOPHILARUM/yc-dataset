# **Why does medical coding matter?**

Medical coding determines how much healthcare providers get paid for their services. Without accurate coding, claims are denied, delaying or preventing payments for the work providers do.

# ​​**Why are we building autonomous medical coding?**

Today, many doctors still handle coding themselves, often relying on Google searches to navigate complex rules they weren't trained for. For example, as one billing manager shared:

"_We've got doctors that like to unbundle stuff, and not understanding that it's bundled, then you can't bill them separately_." 

This challenge becomes even more significant in surgical procedures, where multiple codes, complex bundling rules, and specific documentation requirements create confusion.

Some practices outsource coding and billing, but this approach isn’t perfect either. Coders aren’t in the exam room and often lack the full context of the patient encounter. This leads to time-consuming back-and-forth clarifications with physicians, delaying claims and introducing inaccuracies.

# **Solution**

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=87221&key=user_uploads/198052/642df116-7838-4e4b-92c6-a61052dd417b)

To help providers get started, we've made [ICD10codes.ai](http://ICD10codes.ai) free for everyone to use. Simply insert a diagnosis or your full clinical note to get the most relevant ICD-10 code back. This works for both diagnoses and procedures.

Our complete system goes further: 

* Handles ICD-10, CPT, and DRG while integrating directly with EHRs like EPIC. 
* Flags bundling issues, suggests appropriate modifiers, and validates documentation against payer requirements in real time.

 

This is especially valuable for surgical coding where providers deal with complex bundling rules and specific documentation requirements from payers like UnitedHealthcare’s enhanced requirements.

By combining autonomous coding with our scribing product, Ember Copilot helps providers identify missing documentation and meet requirements while the patient is still in the room. It’s like having a coding expert present at all times—without any overhead or delays.

# **Problem**

There are many AI scribes, but they’re mostly built for outpatient settings. Surgeons work in fast-paced, high-stress environments and rely heavily on dictation, templates, and human scribes. AI makes too many mistakes, and surgeons don’t have the time to fix those inaccuracies.

# **Solution: A medical assistant designed for surgeons**

1. **Quality:** By grounding the documentation in medical history and clinical consultations, we ensure precise SOAP and procedure notes.
2. **Revenue Impact:** Our AI assistant reviews billing history and drafts letters of medical necessity, helping early customers generate over $60,000 per month in additional revenue.
3. **Customization:** We customize workflows to fit subspecialties and fully integrate with general, orthopedic, and other types of surgery.
