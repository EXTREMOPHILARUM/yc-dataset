# **TL;DR**

One of the key reasons **fleets struggle to electrify** is **charging issues**, and **EV charger usage** is **painfully low**. Curo’s platform helps EV charger owners lease their charging spots during low usage times to fleets when they need them most. # 🤯 **Problem: Electrification faces two huge challenges**

1. 🚘 **Fleets** **(especially distributed ones)** **struggle** **to electrify at scale**: EV chargers can cost thousands of dollars and take years to install, even then installation may not be feasible due to land ownership and operating model. It’s really hard. 
2. 🔌 **EV charger usage** **and** **return on investment is painfully** **slow**: private chargers can’t be monetized due to security and liability barriers, with public chargers not being set up for long-term, exclusive use.

By 2030, there are expected to be 9 million electric vehicles in fleets, which, together with public vehicles, requires support from an estimated 40 million new EV chargers. We currently have well under 1 million installed. That’s pretty daunting.

EV chargers have to be used way more effectively for us to stand a chance at supporting millions of electric fleet vehicles.

# 🛟 **Solution: Curo - The Virtual Depot Platform**

Curo’s platform helps EV charger owners lease their charging spaces during low usage times to fleets when they need them most (picture an office building hosting a fleet overnight when employees go home).

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=84703&key=user_uploads/1159251/862f55be-f893-4677-bad3-66b353890cf4)

We make it easy for fleets to identify, plan, and launch at spots they’ve leased and derisk EV charger owners sharing their assets, including legal, security, insurance, and payments.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=84703&key=user_uploads/1159251/51d0e977-5097-4a60-90b8-7461fd08d490)

Curo allows fleets to park and charge anywhere using immediately available and scalable infrastructure, with no upfront cost. We call this a "Virtual Depot” because each site has the utility of a fleet’s traditional depot, distributed in a virtual network.
