**😵‍💫 Problem: Too many subscriptions and too little control**

1. Right now, image and video models and tools are scattered across 10+ platforms (Veo, Kling, Hailuo, Flux, Sora, etc.)
2. The dominant turn-based chat interface makes organizing assets and ideas a mess
3. Generation feels like a slot machine. Trying the same prompt over and over again to get the perfect result is not only expensive but also draining

**😆 Solution: Realtime Collaborative Canvas**

1. All the best image and video models are in one place, with one subscription
2. A Figma-like multiplayer canvas workspace to organize assets, work in parallel, and let ideas flow (no more 2-minute freeze and digging through the chat history)
3. Fine-grained image editing features like inpaint, creative expand, and upscale. So generation no longer feels like pulling a slot machine

## Cekura - Browser Agent That Keeps Your Documentation Up-to-Date 👾

Hey guys! We are Blake and JZ - co-founders of Cekura.

## ⏳TL;DR

Cekura built an AI browser agent that can read how-to articles and navigate the product to verify if the articles are still valid. It saves documentation/customer success teams at SaaS companies hundreds of hours per week.

<https://youtu.be/JJR7hz3dPaQ>

## 😱 Problem: Things Break When You Move Fast

The engineering team is releasing a new feature — hoorayyy! Guess who’s not excited — the documentation team. So many help articles are no longer up-to-date. They are now faced with 2 options:

1. Finding and reading through all the relevant product help articles (this will take literally 100+ hours of manual labor per week for some companies), or
2. Hoping that a dissatisfied customer will file a complaint instead of quietly churning.

## 🤩 Solution: AI Documentation Checker

Cekura’s browser agent will check if your documentation is up-to-date by following the instructions in your documentation like a real user! It can:

1. Set up proper sandbox environment (i.e. admin vs non-admin users)
2. Perform actions like a real person (reading the page, scrolling, clicking, etc)
3. Record session replays to show you exactly what was wrong with the documentation
