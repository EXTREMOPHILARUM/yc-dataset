# Launches

## MediSearch Pro: Your personal medical research assistant

**tl;dr:** We are launching MediSearch Pro—your personal medical research assistant. MediSearch Pro is currently the most accurate publicly-available medical search engine, scoring 94.2% on the United States Medical Licensing Examination (USMLE). Now available on web, iOS, and Android.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=86270&key=user_uploads/544896/e2e6710a-1040-46bb-8d46-51e0e4655daf)

**iOS**: <https://apps.apple.com/us/app/medisearch-ai/id6473300940>\
**Android**: <https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=io.medisearch.twa>\
**Web**: <https://medisearch.io>

**Some highlights**:

* **Industry-Leading Accuracy**: 94.2% accuracy on USMLE
* **Smart Information Extraction**: Automatically highlights key information from medical papers, including summaries and critical statistics
* **Advanced Followups**: One-click access to related papers, contradicting evidence, and real-world discussions
* **Enhanced Filters**: Refined search by publication date and content type

MediSearch Pro is now available globally on web, iOS, and Android. We're committed to making medical information more accessible and accurate for everyone. Please email [founders@medisearch.io](mailto:founders@medisearch.io) to share your feedback! Learn more about MediSearch Pro on our [announcement page](https://medisearch.io/announcements/pro_release).

## MediSearch 👨‍⚕️🔎 AI search engine for trustworthy medical information

**tl;dr:** You know how when you google stuff about your health, you always end up getting diagnosed with some deadly disease by an SEO-optimized health blog? [MediSearch](https://medisearch.io) gives you direct answers to medical questions based on trustworthy sources using LLMs. Also available as an [API](https://medisearch.io/developers).

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=73952&key=user_uploads/593908/132974ad-1386-4c1b-bee4-44c9f38bccdf)

\*\*Story and Problem:\*\*\
Eduard was going to have his 2nd surgery on his knee in December. He visited 8 surgeons in 4 different countries and was surprised to find 6 different surgery options. He had no idea which opinion to trust, so he turned to researching online. We realized that current options for finding health information are either:

1. unable to give direct answers to questions (WebMD and Healthline)
2. untrustworthy (current LLM chatbots or health blogs on Google)
3. or too technical (PubMed papers)

**Solution:**

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=73952&key=user_uploads/593908/da7d8464-0825-4c35-80f8-d25f02ec1675)

Since joining YC, the usage of MediSearch has grown 30x, validating that we are solving a real problem.

Also, to the best of our knowledge, MediSearch is currently the best performing publicly available medical QA system on the United States medical licensing sample exam. You can check our performance [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1miFwLyXdU-RJiWc40ifwmYToAUt0ZqXgXmPW51AYUlo/edit).

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=73952&key=user_uploads/593908/7d6f60e7-80db-4f53-9375-ad775f39660f)

Several YC companies recently reached out whether we offer MediSearch through an API. So now we [do](https://medisearch.io/developers). If you’re interested, reach out to us at [founders@medisearch.io](mailto:founders@medisearch.io).
