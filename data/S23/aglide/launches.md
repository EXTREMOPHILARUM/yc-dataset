# **A lot of apps can’t be managed with SAML**

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=81693&key=user_uploads/496743/55e0ee0d-a31c-48e9-a66e-1e75a1b4cbe7)

SSOs, like Okta, rely on SAML to manage apps. Unfortunately, SAML has a lot of problems.

* **SSO Tax:** Vendors often double or triple their per-head price just to enable SAML (e.g., Figma, Hubspot, [Monday.com](http://monday.com/))
* **Minimum Licenses:** Some vendors set a minimum license count to unlock SAML (e.g., Coursera)
* **Lack of Support:** Smaller or older vendors often don't support SAML at all (e.g., Xero).
* **Account Sharing:** SAML can't be used when you need multiple users to access a single account (e.g., X/Twitter).

As a result, companies are left with apps outside of their SSO.

# **Having apps outside your SSO causes problems**

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=81693&key=user_uploads/496743/6633000d-928b-43f3-bda8-9b0873a1cbee)

* **Security Risks:** Unmanaged accounts are much more likely to be breached (phishing, no conditional access, no 2FA, reused passwords, etc.)
* **Compliance Impacts:** Without proper access management, companies may fail to meet key compliance standards (SOC-II, ISO27001, PCI, HIPPA, etc.).
* **Disjointed End-User Experience:** Users don’t know what they have or how they can access it.
* **Inefficient IT Teams:** Non-SSO accounts are difficult to manage and routinely get forgotten when leavers are off-boarded - resulting in access being kept.

# **Password managers are a bad solution.**

When accounts aren't managed by SAML, they are often stored in password managers, causing headaches:

* **No Access Management:** Password managers can't configure a user's ability to access services (e.g., on company devices only or with a USB Security Key)
* **Off-boarding:** When employees leave, admins might not know what apps they had access to - meaning accounts their accounts won’t get deactivated.
* **Leavers Keeping Access:** Employees take passwords with them when they leave - meaning they can still access company information.
