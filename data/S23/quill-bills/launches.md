## Quill Bills: AI billing that makes doctors more money!

**The Problem**

Right now, every doctor in the US pays for human billers. Some of them outsource it to a billing agency which charges them anywhere from 2% to _10% of total revenue_. (that’s crazy!)

That on its own is expensive, but there’s also another problem: Since processing bills is expensive, up to **30%** of all claim revenue is denied by health insurance companies, and no follow up is made by the biller to fight it.

**The Solution**

Doctors in the US right now are being charged up to 10% on their bottom line and losing up to 30% of their top line revenue.

Well, we’ve figured out how to fix this problem, by making it a lot cheaper to do billing. We’ve figured out how to do it (nearly) all with AI. We have a team of human billers to do manual checks, but they can process orders of magnitude more claims than a biller doing it manually (which is all of them right now).

This means we can not only charge lower fees, but also we have more bandwidth to fight that 30% denial rate! So we typically end up making doctors more money.

We’ve got decades of billing experience on our team, and are already deployed with clinics and hospitals in the US processing millions of dollars in annual claim revenue.

## Quill AI: A 10x more efficient equity research platform

**tl;dr** We are using LLMs to speed up the equity research process by taking the pain out of working with financial filings.

**Problem 1:** **Pulling tables from SEC filings**

The de-facto current solution is a tool called BamSEC. However, there are a couple of problems with it: a) They don’t provide citation links to their numbers, and b) To merge data from previous filings, you can only do it one table at a time.

Quill solves both these problems. Take a look:

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=81746&key=user_uploads/1188332/a0ad69cc-e3d1-44e3-be24-11b74b4c7147)

**Problem 2: PDFs → Excel is painful**

This flow normally involves a lot of number-by-number copy-pasting. Quill lets you skip that and turn any PDF into a spreadsheet (extracting all reported numbers and tables) – with citation links for each number.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=81746&key=user_uploads/1188332/e60269ce-c233-48e5-a639-b4bf03e5d709)

**Problem 3: Earnings season is extremely hectic**

Every quarter, companies report earnings and investors have to quickly digest the performance, guidance, and Q&A sections of the earnings calls for each of their 20-40 portfolio companies before forming an investment opinion.

Quill lets you digest these sections in an instant. Instead of reading through the raw calls, you get a dashboard that explains the main reported performance and guidance metrics, the key points in management’s commentary, and the key concerns of Wall Street analysts.

Faster workflows mean you can cover more companies per quarter.

## Quill AI: The financial research assistant

**TLDR:** We let investors a) extract information from SEC filings just by asking, and b) screen stocks much faster. If you have friends in finance who want to get more sleep this week, send them to [quillai.co](http://quillai.co)!

Quill AI lets you search through SEC filings and screen stocks on command. And we don’t just summarize the information - we point you right to the source, making it easy to audit the results.

**The Problem(s)**

* Investors spend hours reading SEC filings looking for key bits of information to use in their models/reports.
* Existing AI info extraction tools don’t cite sources very well - making them difficult to audit. They also aren’t tuned for financial filings.
* Current stock screeners require an annoying filtering process to produce meaningful lists of investment opportunities.

**The Solution**

* Quill’s web app, which lets you search for key pieces of information across financial documents in Q&A format.
