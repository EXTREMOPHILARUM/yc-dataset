### **Cool…but what’s the problem?**

Imagine watching this footage and transcribing all of the slated information by hand.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=73000&key=user_uploads/1357272/43e60a01-2a4a-4ab2-a5be-7db2bbeec90e)

People have to do this - and much more - to organize the troths of footage that go into a movie, or a commercial, or a trailer, or a YouTube video, or a…you get the point.

The process of manually logging this _metadata_ is expensive and time consuming, and mistakes can be catastrophic.

# What does Kino do?

Kino automatically infers metadata and uses it to label and organize footage, saving hours every day for assistant editors. We transcribe audio and “describe” the video and turn an unstructured mountain of footage into a useful index. And it all happens locally on the editor’s machine.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=73000&key=user_uploads/1357272/f705576a-30de-41fe-ae3a-f74397903714)

Our first feature lets users use natural language to find specific moments within their entire footage library. Check out this [demo video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lwon6fWvmco)!
