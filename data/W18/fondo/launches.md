All Delaware Corporations are legally required to file Delaware Franchise Tax each year (beginning with their year of incorporation)

What often what trips founders up is that Delaware will notify you that you owe $50k+. This is because there are two methods to calculate the total franchise tax owed: the **Authorized Shares Method** and **Assumed Par Value Capital Method**. Delaware gives startups the flexibility to use the method that results in the lessor tax. This gets more complicated once you’ve raised a priced round. Fondo will make sure to file based on the lowest tax method legally possible, generally **Assumed Par Value Capital Method.**
