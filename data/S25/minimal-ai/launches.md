# **Problem**

Everyone thinks AI customer support is solved. It's not. E-commerce customer support is complex and fast-paced. Stores have:

1. Fragmented tech stacks (e.g. Shopify + Zendesk + Shipbob + 12 other tools)
2. Policies that change weekly (return windows, shipping rates, product launches etc.)
3. Brand-specific nuances (tone-of-voice, upsell strategies etc.)

Current solutions force an impossible choice: spend months on expensive implementations with endless vendor calls and complex solutions (that break whenever your business evolves), or settle for basic black-box chatbots that frustrate customers. E-commerce brands can't afford to waste resources on either. Tom Blomfield even called current AI Customer Service tools vaporware:

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=92995&key=user_uploads/1977649/0d7deb74-50db-4652-997e-95150298f702)

# **Solution**

We make it easy for e-commerce brands to build and manage AI customer support teams that resolve 80% of tickets.

**How it works**\
Users train and manage the AI Agents through a single conversational\
interface called the AI Manager. Users can transparently see the Agent's reasoning, provide feedback, and watch it instantly update protocols. The AI Manager also spots gaps and proactively suggests improvements.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=92995&key=user_uploads/1977649/de1a17d0-bca1-4a28-88e2-4aabc24ad8b7)

For example, users might say:

* _"For ticket #12345, use the WMS tracking URL instead of Shopify next time"_
* _"Orders from multiple ShipBob locations arrive separately - warn customers upfront"_
* _"Damaged items need 2 clear photos before refunding"_

The AI Manager instantly updates protocols and asks clarifying questions when needed. Done. Your Agents now handle similar cases perfectly.

It feels like outsourcing your support to a team of AI Agents, with the AI Manager as single point of contact.
