# ❌ **The problem:**

**Drug Discovery is slow:**

* It takes 10+ years and over $2 billion to discover a new drug.
* 2 in 10000 compounds make it.
* Chemistry execution is very slow.

Experiments take time! Designing good experiments and trial and error in the lab are big factors.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=92473&key=user_uploads/2507050/0cd3f202-e48b-48da-a6e5-3ae1572cddab)

**Software for chemists is fragmented:**

* There are many solutions for many different parts of the pipeline.
* Tools are outdated, and integration is largely missing.

**Lab robots are underused:**

* Most lab robots are _not accessible to scientists_ because they are hard to use.
* Drag and drop software for multimillion-dollar robotic labs.
* Lab automation engineers are hard to find.

# ✅ **Our** **Solution:**

We are integrating a full stack of Agents to **design experiments** and **write code for the robot** that runs these experiments, with the tools and context needed to predict chemically meaningful results, and close the experimental loop.\
\
**Key capabilities of our Agents:**

* **Streamline Experiment Planning**: Our tools accelerate creation of experimental details based on literature and expert tools, and tailor the output to the user’s specific needs.
* **Unified platform:** Our platform integrates several agents that are in tight communication and can go from natural language input to robotic execution in minutes.
* **No coding required - use natural language:** With arbitrary input flexibility, we can control the robot the same way you would talk to your lab-mates, including inputs from previous runs, and uploading relevant documents.
* **Parallel and scalable to any lab:** We are vendor agnostic, and integrate with different robotics (Chemspeed, Unchained, Opentrons, etc.) and labs performing experiments manually.

**Pilot Program:** Our team will work on integrating Agentic solutions tailored to your lab's specific problems, whether you use lab automation or not. We want to speed up molecular discovery.
