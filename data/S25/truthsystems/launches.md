## **💬 The Problem**

Law firms, banks, and other firms in regulated industries want to use more AI, but doing so without robust governance tools risks compliance breaches and client trust loss.

The problem is most acute in law firms, which we observed firsthand as AI researchers at Stanford Law School. **It stems from three primary challenges:**

* **Unmanaged Liability:** There is no room for mistakes at law firms. Simple actions, such as uploading confidential files to AI tools or relying on AI-generated citations, can result in millions of dollars' worth of breaches and, more importantly, erode client trust.
* **No Operational Control:** Firms can't see or control how employees use different AI tools, and they have no way to enforce the policies they write. 
* **Conflicting Client Mandates:** One client may require prior written approval for all AI tools, another might forbid their data on a specific matter from leaving the country, and a third bans AI entirely. Manually enforcing these unique rules for every client and their respective matter is impossible.

**The result:** Firms hesitate to fully embrace AI, fearing reputational damage and costly mistakes; many ban AI or have already paid millions for errors discovered too late.

**🏆 The Solution**

Truth Systems provides firms with a compliance AI agent that reads firm policies and client guidelines, then converts them into dynamic guardrails that can be enforced on top of all AI usage
