# **❌ The Problem**

Today’s Counter-Unmanned Aerial System (**CUAS**) solutions are too expensive, too heavy, too slow, and too ineffective.

1. **70-80%** of battlefield fatalities come from drone strikes
2. **$100,000+** kinetic solutions are used to counteract **$500** drones
   * **$500** drones are eliminating **$100M** assets
3. Current solutions are easily defeated with fiber-optic, visual inertial odometry, or simple waypoint navigation missions

# 🚀 \*\* Our Solution \*\*

Perseus is creating the first affordable guided micro-missile pods to bolster American defense capabilities both at home and abroad.

Our micro-guided missiles detect, identify, and eliminate incoming threats before they can reach their intended target. They cost less than $10k, which is 2 orders of magnitude less than the current best solutions.

Steve and I felt we could create a better solution to the CUAS problem and decided we had a moral obligation to follow through on that belief. We quit our old jobs and put it all on the line to fast-track this capability for the U.S. and our allies.

# **The Team**

Jason and Steve studied aerospace engineering together at Penn State University and founded Perseus Defense in 2025.

**Jason (CEO)** designed NASA’s Titan Dragonfly space helicopter, led a NASA advanced projects team to design a next-generation Mars helicopter, and has contributed to multiple aerospace programs across NASA, the DoD, and commercial sectors. He holds a PhD in aerospace engineering with specializations in aircraft design, supercomputing, and machine learning. Jason also taught Aerodynamics at Stanford, speaks four languages, and founded an international drone competition.

**Steve (CTO)** has worked as a swarm robotics researcher focused on building and deploying real-world autonomy. He won swarm robotics competitions with the Office of Naval Research, built autonomy that allows commercial aircraft to land themselves at Boeing, and developed collaborative behaviors for Unmanned Surface Vehicles at Applied Intuition.
