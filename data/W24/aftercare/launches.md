## Aftercare: Conversational AI surveys with intelligent follow-up questions

Hey everyone! We’re [Anand](https://www.linkedin.com/in/anand-nanduri/), [Justin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/justinseanmay/), and [Aidan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/aidan-cj-lee/), co-founders of [Aftercare](https://www.getaftercare.com/).

**TL;DR:** We’re building a generalizable survey tool that uses intelligent AI follow-up questions to gather better insights.

## **🔭 How we got here**

While we were building an eCommerce post-purchase survey platform, one of the most valuable features for our customers was using AI to ask follow-up questions to dig into survey responses and uncover deeper insights—almost like having an interviewer ask good questions live for every survey. We realized this powerful feature could be applied to all surveys, so expanded the survey platform so anyone could use it.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=85495&key=user_uploads/1355492/f34a1950-734e-4956-b96d-4232807f1ff0)

## **🧐 Problem**

* Traditional surveys can’t react to respondent answers and ask follow-up questions, leaving behind incredibly valuable insights. Often, the first response to an open-ended question does not tell the whole story.
* Good open-ended feedback is incredibly useful, but difficult to view in a structured format, especially when you have many responses. If you’ve ever used Google Forms, open-ended responses are presented as a data dump.

## **🚀 Our Solution**

* Easily create surveys, and turn on AI follow-up questions for any topic you want to dig into - Aftercare will ask intelligent follow-up questions automatically.
* AI summaries surface key information from your responses automatically, and open-ended responses are categorized into data visualizations of your choice.

## Aftercare - Conversational post-purchase surveys using AI

Hey everyone! We’re [Anand](https://www.linkedin.com/in/anand-nanduri/), [Justin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/justinseanmay/), and [Aidan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/aidan-cj-lee/), previously co-founders of Kontractify, coming back as [Aftercare](https://www.linkedin.com/company/aftercarecompany/).

**TL;DR:** We help eCommerce stores run conversational post-purchase surveys using AI to capture open-ended customer feedback and marketing attribution data.

# **🍪 Problem**

With the “death of the cookie” rapidly approaching, eCommerce stores must rely more on 1st- and zero-party data to inform marketing decisions like where best to spend money on ads (Meta vs. TikTok vs. Google, etc.).

Because of this, eCommerce stores are more widely adopting tools like post-purchase surveys. However, the current post-purchase survey landscape is lacking in several ways:

* Limited: mainly offers multiple-choice questions that don’t provide enough context to be useful for marketing teams.
* Static: asks the same questions regardless of the user’s segment (e.g., new vs. returning user) or the user’s previous responses within a single survey.
* Disjointed: integrates poorly with the rest of the marketing stack.

# **✨ Solution**

Aftercare is a modern post-purchase survey tool that uses AI to go beyond the traditional multiple-choice survey. It:

* Asks open-ended questions to capture more detailed and candid feedback.
* Changes what it asks based on the user’s previous responses so you can ask smart follow-up questions in real time.
* Summarizes all insights in a single dashboard to inform product and marketing teams at a glance.

Our MVP is a Shopify App, but we want to meet merchants and customers where they’re at, so we’re building out other channels, including a generalized web Iframe, SMS, and voice.

## Kontractify - Painless RFPs, powered by LLMs 📢

Hey everyone! We’re [Justin](https://linkedin.com/in/justinseanmay), [Anand](https://linkedin.com/in/anand-nanduri), and [Aidan](https://linkedin.com/in/aidan-cj-lee) – childhood friends and co-founders of Kontractify.

**TL;DR** [Kontractify](https://www.kontractify.com/) uses LLMs to help enterprises run RFPs. We generate questions, manage workflows, evaluate proposals, and facilitate reverse auctions.

## 🧨 **The Problem**

Enterprises buy large amounts of goods and services through RFPs—competitive bid processes where they invite multiple sellers/service providers to participate. These buyers launch RFPs on extremely outdated platforms, often costing hundreds of thousands of dollars, and have UI/UX stuck in the ‘90s.

Buyers get so frustrated with these tools that they ignore using them altogether and revert to Excel spreadsheets and emails. Wasted time, frustration, information loss, and lack of user participation are common.

On the other side, sellers who receive RFP notifications hate filling out responses on these platforms, leading many to not participate.

## 🎉 **Our Solution**

Kontractify provides a painless experience for enterprise buying teams to run and collaborate on RFPs, using LLMs to speed up particularly tedious processes. Buyers can build, launch, and manage all seller communications through the platform.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=79017&key=user_uploads/1355492/bed9d3f3-1454-460b-957d-a46f3630192a)

**For Buyers:**

* ⚙️ Simple workflow and question builders help buyers painlessly curate events
* ✍️ Tedious activities like writing question lists are automated away with the help of LLMs
* 📣 Advanced eBidding events such as Reverse Auctions can be easily launched, allowing for greater savings

**For Sellers:**

* 📫 RFP questions can be responded to directly by email and LLMs assist in converting the unstructured text into a templated response

⭐️ Consumer-grade UI and LLMs greatly improve velocity for both buyers and sellers. Buyers can develop new supplier relationships, run more events, and save more money. Sellers can quickly respond to RFPs and focus on their next sale.
