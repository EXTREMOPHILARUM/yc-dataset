# **The Problem**

Every fast-growing company faces the same challenge: enterprise deals are blocked until you get SOC 2, HIPAA, or other certifications. Yet the traditional compliance process is time-consuming, complex, and annoying:

* Engineering teams waste hundreds of hours manually setting configurations
* Admin teams get overwhelmed with screenshots and evidence gathering
* **Deals get delayed or lost while scrambling to pass audits**

In short, companies spend weeks manually documenting policies, collecting evidence across dozens of tools, and going back-and-forth with auditors—all while crucial enterprise deals hang in the balance.

# **Solution**

Delve is an AI-native compliance platform that eliminates hours of manual work typically required by traditional platforms. While other solutions are built on API integrations and require teams to manually collect screenshots of security configurations, Delve simplifies this: customers write a single instruction and AI agents automatically collect the required evidence.

**→ To eliminate manual work,** our AI agents collect evidence from web apps, internal tools, and custom software—going beyond what API integrations are capable of. This means you can automate a much broader scope of compliance requirements, capturing everything from password policies to access controls and employee training records.

**→ For continuous protection,** our platform monitors across your entire company, updating evidence in real-time and alerting team members of compliance gaps before they affect security.

**→ For enterprise scale,** we support SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO 27001, GDPR, PCI DSS, and custom frameworks—with more certifications coming soon. Our customers have passed even the most rigorous enterprise security reviews, unlocking deals worth up to $100M annually.

### What is HIPAA Compliance?

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) outlines how sensitive health data should be handled in the US. If you’re a company that processes health data, you most likely need to become HIPAA compliant.

It’s important to take HIPAA seriously. The consequences of violating it can be severe – from high fines (up to $1.5M per year) to criminal prosecution.
