## **The Problem**

CRMs like Salesforce and HubSpot are only useful when populated with really good data. Without good data, pretty much every CRM feature like reporting, forecasting, automation, etc. becomes either useless or actively harmful. The problem is that updating the CRM with good data is **hard**, and **traditional methods don’t work.**

\
There’s two main ways CRMs get updated today:

1. A rep manually writes notes, updates deal stages, fills in custom fields, and generally does a whole lot of data entry.
2. A CRM integration ‘captures’ activity from email/calendar and copy pastes it into the CRM automatically.

But _both of these fail_. Why?

CRM integrations don’t organize data. They copy-paste unstructured data into your CRM, but to be _useful_ data needs to be structured & organized into records (new contacts, deal stage updates, intelligent notes, custom field changes, etc). Activity capture is not good at that.

Reps can organize data into records, but, like many highly manual & repetitive things, they _hate_ it and are bad at it. Human CRM updates become very error-prone and inconsistent (or nonexistent).

**The Solution**

<https://youtu.be/WtH2W81lnQs>

We create AI agents that constantly monitor your emails, calendar, calls, or wherever else you have relationship data and organize it into CRM records as meticulously as a great rep would.

This looks like enriching and creating contacts, writing detailed & specific notes, associating related records, updating deal stages, scoring leads, etc.

The agents work in the background — you never have to touch our UI or touch your CRM; just work as you would normally and our agents keep track of everything.

We integrate with over 50 business apps and CRMs like Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, and Zoho. We add new integrations quickly!

Our agents are tailored to your deal process and the information you want to record. 

* For example, one of our B2B SaaS customers really cares about creating notes after calls with information like what connectors an enterprise is using and whether they’re self-hosting or not. They want the notes associated with the correct opportunity, account, & all stakeholder contacts.
* On the other hand, financial services firms we work with have tons of domain-specific custom fields, custom objects, and deal stages they want to populate from email exchanges.

Since our agents follow arbitrary plain English instructions, we can accommodate almost every deal process.
