# How it works

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=86073&key=user_uploads/635063/356232f6-9e59-4216-afa8-b0f7c808b541)

[The Drop](https://www.getdrop.ai) uses **computer vision and AI** to know when you're eating food. Once the smart AI chip on the device classifies something as food, the LED light turns on and the device starts recording what you're eating. Afterwards, the cropped image of the meal will be sent to the cloud for analysis. The algorithms will then break down your meal into **calories, protein, carbs and fats**, which you could see right away in the app on your phone.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=86073&key=user_uploads/635063/3d7365f6-f033-44b4-9867-59268af954c8)

# Why are we building this?

We launched [Rex Nutribot on WhatsApp](https://www.rex.fit) in December 2023. We made calorie tracking as simple as sending a photo or a text to a friend. More than 20,000 users later, we learned that nutrition tracking is still not a solved problem. Every solution on the market requires **too much input from the user**, and most don't manage to create the habit to track what they eat. 

A real solution needs to be as **user-friendly and intuitive** as fitness tracking is. It needs to be a wearable that you can just put on in the morning and forget about. For that reason we developed The Drop: the first fully automated nutrition tracker.

By fully automating meal tracking we want to **enable everyone to learn what and how much they eat**, understand their eating habits and transform their diet to optimize for health and longevity.

## Babylog: A lightweight open-source logging library for computer vision

Hi everyone,

We’re excited to launch our new **open-source logging library for ML and computer vision teams**. With Babylog, we aim to empower engineers to **easily log raw image and prediction data from edge devices to the cloud (or on-prem servers).**

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=68843&key=user_uploads/635063/04dbb932-16fa-4e04-bc2c-b7a14f273ebf)

## Problem

You need to know what your models are running on in the real world. Setting up that infrastructure takes away precious time and money. You need a standard for logging your data so that you know how your models perform. **Babylog (bah-bee-log) provides a standard where all device, model, and prediction information are coupled.**

* If you don’t have a data stream from production, you have no idea what your models are seeing and no way to evaluate their performance.
* If something suddenly changes in the real world and with it the distribution of your inputs, things could go very wrong so you’d likely want to know (i.e. stop signs suddenly turning blue = Tesla becoming a murder machine)
