## Problem

Common Paper started by creating [standard agreements](https://commonpaper.com/standards/) that have been signed by more than 10,000 companies and used to close more than $100 million dollars in deals. We also make contract management software for customizing, signing, and tracking agreements with customers and partners.

As companies scale, more and more of their sales teams’ time is spent dealing with contract redlines from their customers.  After talking seeing the problem close up at countless companies, we found that most folks fall into one of two camps:

* Every contract redline gets sent to their attorney, which is expensive and adds delays
* Nothing gets sent to the attorney, and the founder, sales, ops, or other non-lawyer handles all redlines. This creates a significant risk when they don’t know what they don’t know about risks in contracts

A better approach is to have AI do a first read of each contract and then triage the redlines so that as much as possible is handled automatically, and then if anything remains, assign it to the right person. That’s what we built with Gerri.

## Solution

There are three ways contracts get into Gerri:

* Upload
* Forward an email with attachment
* Have Gerri scan your team’s inboxes for redlined contracts and automatically review them as they come in

Once Gerri gets a contract, it compares it to your playbook, which contains rules about what you will and won’t accept, how you respond to asks, comments to leave in the doc, etc. Gerri includes a demo playbook to help you get started, and you can chat or answer questions to have Gerri help you generate your own custom rules.

Gerri then either

* Responds with a clean contract that’s ready to send back to the other side.  Gerri automatically accepts, rejects, counter-proposes, and leaves comments for the other side.  
* Flags which redlines can’t be handled automatically, and notifies the right people for them to weigh in. For example, it might be your lawyer for indemnity issues, CS lead for renewal terms, and IT for special security procedures/certifications.

# Background

We (Jake and Ben) met while working together at a BI startup called RJMetrics. Jake was a co-founder, and Ben was an engineering leader. At that job and others, we were incredibly **frustrated by contract negotiation**. The process was filled with:

* **⏳ Unpredictable delays**
* **💰 Huge legal bills**
* **🥽 Extremely limited visibility**

We realized that founders and go-to-market teams have many of the same problems with sales that were widespread in startup fundraising before the introduction of the SAFE. Each company has a unique sales or procurement contract (and NDA, DPA, etc).  People argue over whose template to use, and **no matter who wins that argument, one side of the deal has to review and negotiate a new contract for the first time**.

The same basic ideas show up in all contracts about a given type of transaction, but they are expressed totally differently.  As an example, take governing law, one of the simplest terms in most contracts.  These are real examples from four contracts, showing four different ways describe governing law:

# Standard Contracts & Structured Data

To solve this, we looked to the SAFE and other widely used contracts for inspiration and created standard, open-source contracts for buying and selling software, including:

* [Design Partner Agreement](https://commonpaper.com/standards/design-partner-agreement/)
* [Mutual NDA](https://commonpaper.com/standards/mutual-nda/)
* [Terms of Service](https://commonpaper.com/standards/terms-of-service/)
* [Cloud Service Agreement](https://commonpaper.com/standards/cloud-service-agreement/) 
* [Data Processing Agreement](https://commonpaper.com/standards/data-processing-agreement/), [Service Level Agreement](https://commonpaper.com/standards/service-level-agreement/), and more

These contracts have been downloaded by thousands of companies, are created by a committee of attorneys, and are free to use under a Creative Commons license.
