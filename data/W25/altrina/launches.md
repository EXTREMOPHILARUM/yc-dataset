## Altrina Automator: Turn demonstrations into reliable browser automations in 5 minutes

# **The Problem:**

Every automation tool promises the same thing: demo it once, automate it forever. One-click, reliable, non-technical.

None of them actually work. They either:

* **Are unreliable** - Break when websites change, fail silently, or work inconsistently
* **Are too complex** - Require literal “online universities” to learn
* **Are still technical** - Building, debugging, and maintaining automations still requires technical expertise

We know because we tried them all. Then failed to build it ourselves. Twice.

So we did something different: spent 9 months as automation consultants, building workflows by hand for real customers across manufacturing, legal, sales, and operations. Not demos. Not slides. Just us using our own platform to build automations that had to work every day and generate real value.

After 9 months, we're confident: **it works**.

# **Our Solution:**

Altrina Automator lets you create automations by simply demonstrating them:

1. **Record** - Turn on our Chrome extension and perform your workflow once
2. **Review** - Check the recorded steps, add specifications in natural language
3. **Generate** - Click one button to create your automation
4. **Test** - Test run it and adjust anything
5. **Schedule / Scale** - Set up triggers (daily, weekly, or custom) OR run thousands of automations in parallel using our spreadsheet interface

Everything is editable in natural language—no code required.

**Our Story 📖**

Harvey worked on deep learning models for recommendation systems at TikTok and Google, improving the experience for billions of people worldwide. Mo worked on self-driving cars at NVIDIA for 4.5 years, inventing self-optimizing motion controllers with RL and filing 4 patents in the process.

Much like a self-driving car, AI agents perceive the world, plan a sequence of actions, and act on that plan. We came together last year to build a state-of-the-art web browsing agent that **achieves 93% on WebVoyager, outperforming OpenAI Operator, Google’s Project Mariner, and all other browsing agents publicly available.** 

For an AI agent to take actions on your behalf, **the bar for accuracy is high**, especially for enterprises. While ordering a pizza to a random location 400 miles away is something to laugh about, hallucinating facts in a consultant’s research report is a _very_ big deal.

AI agents act exactly like human workers who’ve never experienced a particular task: neither get it perfectly right the first time. Humans, however, _learn_ from demonstration, experience, and feedback.

This learning ability is the missing piece towards human-level AI. We’re building a [novel cognitive architecture](https://www.heytessa.ai/blog/introducing-large-neurosymbolic-cognitive-models) that enables Tessa to not just accomplish tasks like a human, but also learn with experience and natural language feedback. This architecture enables information retrieval that is **guaranteed hallucination-free**.
