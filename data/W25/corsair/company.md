# Corsair

> Integration Layer for AI Agents 🦞

Don't do work yourself because you're nervous to hand it off.

AI agents are genuinely capable now, but giving them the keys to your email, Slack, GitHub, and CRM feels reckless. One misunderstood instruction and they're sending emails to the wrong person, creating calendar invites, or pushing code you didn't mean to ship. So you end up doing the work yourself anyway.

Corsair is a drop-in integration layer for AI agents that holds your credentials in an encrypted local database and enforces a configurable permission layer on every call. Anything risky gets paused and sent to you as a review link. Your agent cannot get around it. The approval is enforced in code, not in the prompt.

It works alongside OpenClaw, NanoClaw, or any agent you're already running. Drop it in, configure how much trust each integration gets, and hand off the work you've been too nervous to assign.

It ships with 12 integrations today, including Slack, GitHub, Gmail, Linear, HubSpot, and Google Calendar, with 15 more in development. Multi-tenancy and webhook handlers are built in.

Take a look at the repo and see if it fits how you work: github.com/corsairdev/corsair
