# Launches

## Pickle - AI Body Double for Video Calls

# **tl;dr**

Pickle replaces your Zoom camera with a virtual body double that always looks great—even if you're calling in from bed.

# **Why we’re doing this**

Daniel attended 10‑hour Zoom classes daily when he was a medical school student. He experienced extreme Zoom fatigue and discovered that it wasn’t just because he hated listening to boring medicine lectures—it also caused [neurophysiological](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-45374-y) problems. We imagined there must be [countless people](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-zoom-video-commn-encryption-idUSKBN22C1T4/) suffering from the same issue, desperate to escape the Zoom prison.

<https://youtu.be/cCdZX6hxJpI?si=f3RCtpSQiquAbmCs>

# **Who’s using Pickle?**

Since launching Pickle last October, we’ve interviewed over 400 Picklers worldwide. What surprised us was that not only were people experiencing Zoom fatigue from back-to-back meetings, but even those **who wouldn’t turn on their camera** for a single call were using Pickle.

Engineer moms who have to care for their kids, new employees with severe anxiety disorder, and CEOs who want to look professional no matter where they are—all these people found Pickle essential for their daily work. We realized that Pickle isn’t just a vitamin—**it’s a painkiller**.

# **The lowest latency human model**

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=87621&key=user_uploads/2376609/95f16234-6efd-417b-880f-b00310ccbdbd)

We developed an ultra-fast human model that processes voice input and generates output in **under 0.3 seconds**. This enables a user’s Pickle to lip-sync almost simultaneously with what they say. Moreover, as our user base grows, our model rapidly upgrades thanks to the influx of data.
