## Mercura: Quotes in minutes, not days

# **📜 Background:**

Co-founder Lukas Bock grew up in a family business with over 100 years in plumbing and electrical trades. He’s seen first-hand how manual quoting in the construction industry is.

Inspired by this legacy, Mercura is now using AI to transform how millions of sales reps at distributors & manufacturers work.

# **❌ Problem:**

When contractors bid on construction projects, they request quotes from distributors and manufacturers, often in unstructured formats like emails, Excel files, and PDFs. These requests contain functional product requirements but frequently lack details.

Distributors and manufacturers employ massive inside sales teams that manually interpret these requests, compare specifications with their product catalog, and select suitable products.

This slow, manual, and error-prone process holds off inside sales teams from their core task—selling—while delaying responses to customers and increasing the risk of lost revenue.

<https://youtu.be/Bn-ENaj_6Tc>

# **🪄 Solution:**

Mercura automates sales quotations for HVAC, electrical and plumbing distributors & manufacturers.

By eliminating repetitive back-office tasks with AI, our software enables sales teams to process RFQs in minutes instead of days. This lets them focus on winning more deals and driving revenue.

Our customers use Mercura to quote significantly more requests automatically, which allows them to maximize their sales potential and win more customers.

This is how it works:

1. **Customer request intake** – Requests arrive in various formats (PDFs, Excel, emails, etc.) and must be processed into the ERP.
2. **Data extraction** – Our AI extracts relevant product information, such as product descriptions, types, manufacturers, part numbers, and quantities, and structures it correctly.
3. **Validation & matching** – Our AI matches line items with the distributor’s product catalog, and suggests the best matches based on the customer’s sales history.
4. **ERP entry** – The quote is automatically entered into the ERP.
