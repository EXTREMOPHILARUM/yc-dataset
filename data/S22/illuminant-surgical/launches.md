**❌ The Problem: Patient Access Is Stuck In The 1800s**

Every year, hundreds of millions of procedures require precise access to internal anatomy. Whether placing a screw in the spine, inserting a needle for a biopsy, or advancing a catheter, precision is critical. Yet surgeons still rely on outdated tools: fluoroscopy, “feel”, and mental reconstruction, leading to inefficiencies, complications, and billions in unnecessary healthcare costs.

This has serious consequences:

* For patients – misplaced implants, vascular injuries, and nerve damage result in repeat procedures, extended hospital stays, and life-altering complications.
* For hospitals – preventable surgical revisions cost tens of billions annually in unnecessary expenses
* For surgeons – Cumbersome tools and methods that extend case time, increase cognitive load, and limit the number of patients they can treat.

Spine surgery and interventional access are two of the fastest-growing segments and also among the most accuracy-dependent.

In the US alone:

* 1.6 M spine surgeries where millimeters can mean the difference between mobility and disability.
* 9.5 M interventional procedures that rely on accurate percutaneous access to treat and diagnose hundreds of different conditions and diseases.

**This is a $10B+ unmet need.**

**🔎 Our Solution: See Inside The Patient in Real Time, Directly On The Body**

We’ve built a new way of seeing inside the human body: Skylight is the first real-time anatomical projection system built to solve the problems with patient access. Instead of forcing doctors to look away at a screen, Skylight projects anatomy directly onto the patient, like an AR display—but without headsets, wearables, or extra steps.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=65365&key=user_uploads/636938/8f98ebe8-aa8b-44e5-aac5-1b03d5105bfd)

To make this happen, we’ve developed a novel method of tracking the patient with sub-millimeter accuracy and we’ve built this system to be universally compatible with industry-standard tools and scanners to allow adoption without changing workflow.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=65365&key=user_uploads/636938/d4213fc6-0747-4211-8bc3-56a309fd14c9)

**Just as fiber optics revolutionized how we do laparoscopy and minimally invasive surgeries, anatomical projection will become the new standard for patient access.**
