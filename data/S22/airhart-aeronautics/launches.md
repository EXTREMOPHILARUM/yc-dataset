# Launches

## ✈️ Airhart Sling: The first fly-by-wire personal airplane

At Airhart, we are building airplanes anyone can fly. We’ve been working on our vehicle for close to a year now and are thrilled to announce we are working with Sling Aircraft in developing our first fully fly-by-wire and easy-to-fly airplane: the **Airhart Sling**!

![](https://uploads-ssl.webflow.com/64085db05268829a421ce823/6462978da8bca97ce08640ac_OUTDOOR%20A%20RED.png)

**Why are we building airplanes?**

Common small planes today look, feel, and fly the same way a car from the 1960’s looks, feels, and drives because they were designed and built in that same time. Today, many people don’t see themselves being able to drive a 1960’s manual transmission car, much less a plane from the same era.

Learning the motor skills required to fly a plane today like this adds time, costs, and training complexity. Around [70 to 80%](https://download.aopa.org/epilot/2011/AOPA_Research-The_Flight_Training_Experience.pdf) of aspiring pilots drop out before getting their Private Pilot License citing these as reasons.

**What is the Airhart Sling?**

Based on the [Sling TSi](https://slingaircraft.com/aircraft/sling-tsi/), it will possess all the excellent features typical of a TSi:

* capacity for four people (including the pilot)
* a cruise speed of 148 KTAS,
* a range of 800 nautical miles
* only burning 7.4 gallons of fuel per hour.

The Airhart Sling will feature Airhart’s easy-to-fly controls, creating a safe and user-friendly airplane that anyone can fly.

Read more about the Airhart Sling on our website: <https://www.airhartaero.com/airhart-sling>
