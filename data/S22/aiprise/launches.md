Say goodbye to cumbersome onboarding processes and welcome a streamlined approach to business verification.

### **Why?**

Imagine you want to onboard a business on your platform. Here is what the traditional verification setup looks like - 

**Step 0:** Design a complicated Typeform (Requires 7-10 days)

**Step 1:** Send a Google / Typeform link to the business/merchant to collect the following information about the company -

* Name & Tax ID
* Legal Information
* UBO Information / Ownership Structure
* Website Information
* Registration documents 
* Address Proof
* UBO Identity documents

**Step 2:** Semi-automated validation of the above information (4-5 days)

* Lookup business Tax ID and name in the local business registry
* Order OFAC/AML/Sanctions/Adverse Media checks on the business
* Document verification / KYC checks on each UBO + Legal Representative
* Order OFAC/AML/Sanctions/Adverse Media checks on each UBO
* Manually verify address proof
* Follow up with customer for any missing information

**Step 3:** Risk Analysis (0.5-1 hour)

* Manually go through the website
* Lookup business information and employee count on LinkedIn
* Google search of the business to get insights on customer reviews/news articles, etc.
* Manually go through provided documents and completely rely on intuition to spot fraudulent documents

**Challenges of Traditional Verification:**

* Time-consuming
* Relies on inconsistent data sources
* Difficulty scaling with diverse regulations across multiple geographies
* Prone to fraud due to manual verification errors

### **⚠️ Problem**

Does your platform require you to know who your customers are (aka - KYC / identity verification)?

People who have hunted for KYC/KYB providers in the past are probably already thinking - “Oh ok - here goes one more KYX provider”.

_Hint: We aren’t yet another KYX provider_

True identity verification is super challenging. **No one** provider is usually sufficient to meet all your needs. Each country has different compliance requirements, each platform has a different risk appetite, and simply checking the KYC boxes is usually not enough to prevent real fraud on a platform.

On an average, a company ends up integrating with **10+ identity providers** over time. Layering multiple vendors is not just more fraud proof, but also cost effective in certain cases.

Interesting stuff we heard from other companies:

1. **Cost Optimization** Local providers tend to be cheaper for a given region, but have limited coverage. Use a local provider for requests from a certain country, if it fails - fall back to a more expensive global provider.
2. **Device Attributes:** Regular KYC checks have become too easy for fraudsters. In one case, fraudulent users gained access to legitimate IDs, but the attack was thwarted as all the requests came in with a specific screen resolution set by a bot farm.

We can go on and on about the list here, But the gist is that on an average, a company ends up writing **100+ rules** using a varied combination of APIs just to prevent account opening scams.

Think about the complexity of the decision engine over time and the resources it takes to maintain them!\
