**The scenario:** 

Congratulations! your B2B startup has its first customer ready to buy! What happens next? Your customer sends you a Cloud Services Agreement. This 30-page document is dense with complex legal terms.

You might be tempted to just sign this deal and get the revenue now. The problem is that there are often very harmful, and sometimes even company-ending, terms buried in the first turn of an enterprise contract. Some of these terms are so aggressive that VCs won’t even count the contract toward your ARR. You need a lawyer to find and negotiate those out.

At this point, most founders ask the same law firms that did their incorporation for help. These firms will charge $500–$2000 per hour, won’t estimate how many hours they’ll need to do the job, and will take days or even weeks to turn around your agreement.

What your lawyers are doing behind the scenes is a shockingly inefficient process of passing work between partners and associates over email, unassisted by AI or technology generally. It’s a highly manual process where “data-driven” means “whatever the partner thinks they saw on the last five deals.” If you look closely at the work product, you’re quite likely to [find embarrassing errors](https://general.legal/blog/your-lawyer-is-worse-than-you-think).

In short, you’re paying a lot for a poor result, delivered slowly. In the age of LLMs, this is inexcusable.
