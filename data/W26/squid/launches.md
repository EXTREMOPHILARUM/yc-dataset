# Launches

## Squid 🦑 AI-powered grid planning in your browser

**TL;DR:** Grid decisions still get made across emails, spreadsheets, PDFs, GIS exports, and conflicting model versions. [Squid](https://www.linkedin.com/company/squid-energy/) ([squid.energy](http://squid.energy)) gives utilities a single, trusted model (a live, versioned grid map of substations and lines, with evidence attached) so planning is consistent, and decisions ship faster.

### **The problem**

Most grid organisations have the tools for analysis, but the _decision trail_ is fragmented:

* models live in specialist tools
* evidence lives in decks/docs/spreadsheets
* assumptions live in people’s heads

Result: slow planning cycles, endless “which version is right?”, and hard-to-defend decisions.

### **Our solution**

Squid is a workspace where the **model of the electric grid at the center**.

* **Unify** messy grid data into one coherent model (and keep it current)
* **Version** everything so changes are trackable and comparable
* **Attach evidence + assumptions** to the model, where decisions are made
* **Run repeatable workflows** for planning, connections, and network change

### **Why now**

Electrification is accelerating and the grid is the bottleneck. The old workflow - files, handoffs, static reports - doesn’t scale to the pace of change.
