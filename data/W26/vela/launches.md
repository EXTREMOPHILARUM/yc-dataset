**🧩 The Problem** \
Most scheduling tools assume the world is perfect. They expect rigid time slots and instant replies. But that is not how real business happens. Real business is "Let's do early next week." Real business is a VIP client needing a slot over an internal catch-up. Real business is someone ghosting you for a week. Currently, we have three bad options:

* **The Manual Way:** Personal but a massive time-sink that distracts from deep work.
* **The "Calendly" Way:** Efficient but impersonal. It signals "my time is more important than yours" and breaks the moment you have complex constraints.
* **The Human EA:** Great, but costs $80k/year and isn't available 24/7.

**💡 The Solution** \
Now there's Vela. It is an autonomous agent built for the messy reality of coordination. It doesn't just book slots; it **negotiates** on your behalf with the white-glove service of a human executive assistant.

**How it works:**

* **The "Just CC" Workflow:** Write your message naturally: _"Looping in Vela to find a time for us next week."_ Vela parses the conversation, checks your calendar, and reaches out to the other person directly to negotiate a slot. 
* **It Knows What’s Important To You:** Vela understands social hierarchy. It knows to prioritize a client over a coffee chat without you setting specific rules.
* **It Handles Ambiguity:** You don't need to give it rigid parameters. Tell Vela "Friday evenings are free," and it understands exactly what that means.
