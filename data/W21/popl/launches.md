# 🔥 **The Problem**

Companies (and startups, see data below!) spend **$20K–$1M+** per booth at conferences.

They send their best reps and team members. They fly the team out, book hotels, pay for meals, etc. They invest in beautiful booths and marketing.

But when it comes to capturing leads, most still rely on:

* Clunky badge scanner apps that barely work
* Writing info down in Apple notes or collecting LinkedIns
* CSVs from event organizer delivered _weeks_ after the event
* No CRM integrations meaning slow speed to lead/follow-ups

The result?

**Slow follow-ups (leading to your competitors winning more), lost leads, and lower ROI on events.**

What if there was a platform that solved all of this, that GTM teams could set up once and then use across every single event…

# 🚀 **Our Solution**

Popl provides your company with a unified in-person GTM platform that:

* Scans any event badge, business card, or LinkedIn QR with the same app every time.
* Enriches each scan with accurate contact + company data (work email, phone numbers, LinkedIn, company revenue, tech stack, HQ location, etc.)
* Allows you to add context like notes, qualifying questions, and tags to each lead.
* Syncs it automatically to your CRM in seconds — HubSpot, Salesforce, Marketo, Zoho, Dynamics, Monday, Slack, etc all natively supported.
* Manage everything through event campaigns which also show leaderboards, pipeline generated, and ROI.

**All while your team members stay focused on their conversations, not the tools. Plus no more waiting for CSV uploads, manual data entry, or other post-event headaches.**

# **🤔 Is This For Us?**

Now some might assume events are only for big, legacy companies and not for startups or mid-market companies. But here’s what we’re seeing:

1️⃣ With AI flooding channels like cold email and cold calling, in-person GTM is surging. People still want to buy from people (and especially not from AI), and when digital outreach feels saturated and impersonal, meeting potential buyers face-to-face gives you a real edge.

2️⃣ The GTM data on startup companies shared by [@Richard Lee](https://www.linkedin.com/in/zrlee/) showed a hidden insight that “Attending conferences” is the 3rd most popular growth strategy for YC startups. This shows events are not just a nice-to-have, they are a core growth lever.

3️⃣ We’re already seeing modern, fast-growing companies adopt Popl — like Square, Hammerspace, MavenAGI, Turing, Vercel, and Wiz. Events aren’t just for legacy enterprises; they’re becoming a key channel for the next generation of breakout startups.
