# **Insurance Claims Processing is Manual and Slow**

Insurance adjusters are drowning in manual work to process claims. This creates delays, errors, and frustrated policyholders while burying adjusters in tedious work instead of decision-making. Adjusters face three major bottlenecks:

* **Fragmented communication:** Adjusters spend hours fielding routine calls and emails to gather and relay claim information. Phone tag with claimants, vendors, and internal teams creates delays and frustration.
* **Slow document review:** Sifting through unstructured documents, photos, and reports to validate evidence is time-intensive. Manual review of complex documentation slows case resolution.
* **Tedious record keeping:** Converting notes and decisions into structured data for claims management systems requires constant manual data entry.

An aging workforce exacerbates these issues, creating a talent shortage as workloads grow. With median adjuster compensation already at $109k and razor-thin margins for carriers and TPAs, the economics of manual processing are unsustainable.
