## Sygaldry: Quantum-Accelerated AI Servers

We’re Chad Rigetti and Idalia Friedson, and we’re building Sygaldry. Chad Rigetti is in second-time founder mode with Sygaldry, having built quantum computing company Rigetti Computing from YC S14 to a public company (NASDAQ: RGTI). Idalia Friedson, former Chief Strategy Officer at Strangeworks, helped Chad take Rigetti public in 2022 after starting the world’s first quantum policy center at the Hudson Institute.

**Why Sygaldry?**

Sygaldry’s quantum-accelerated AI servers will allow companies to innovate more quickly, improve margins, and deploy better AI products by providing speedups at a fraction of the cost and energy of GPUs alone.

https://youtu.be/X7NxwNPHpQM

**The Problem:**

AI progress demands more chips, more power, and more data centers—a **trajectory that is physically and environmentally unsustainable** with purely classical infrastructure.

**The Solution:**

Augment classical infrastructure with quantum processors built for AI, since quantum can provide exponential speedups for specific AI tasks. **Sygaldry is building quantum-accelerated AI servers to speed up training and inference and creating tools for AI researchers to integrate quantum capabilities into AI models and workflows.**

**The Impact:**

* Reduce the time between model releases
* Fine-tune existing models faster
* Enable faster token generation for distributed LLMs
* Unlock faster inference for diffusion models
* Conduct greater exploration in novel model architecture
* Leverage secure quantum communication channels
* Increase AI affordability, accessibility, and personalization

**Sygaldry’s Unique Approach:**

Most quantum computers are built using one type of quantum bit, but each qubit has distinct strengths and weaknesses. **Sygaldry AI servers combine multiple qubit types within a single, fault-tolerant architecture to leverage their strengths, while avoiding their limitations.** This is the same way classical computers are made. We believe this approach to quantum will seem obvious in retrospect.
