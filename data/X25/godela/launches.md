# Launches

## Godela: AI Powered Physics Engine for Engineers

**AI powered physics is the next frontier**

**TL;DR:** Godela is an AI-powered physics engine that gives engineers faster, cheaper, simulation-quality answers to even the most complicated engineering problems.

Hi YC—we’re @Cinnamon Sipper and @Abhijit Pamarty, former engineers at Apple, Google, and Intel, now turning our Stanford research into the future of engineering.

https://youtu.be/I5E1Zi9cpM4

**The Problem: Engineering Discovery is Too Slow.** Every groundbreaking innovation—from fusion reactors to life-saving drugs—hinges on understanding how the physical world works. Yet, engineers spend agonizing weeks, even months, wrestling with complex simulations, preprocessing data, and manually tuning parameters just to answer _one_ question. Many critical questions go unasked because the effort is too great.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=90799&key=user_uploads/1725296/03894dba-703c-4a25-a00b-b1ba24b27392)

**Our Vision: Ask About the Physical World as Easily as You Use Language.** Imagine a world where understanding the implications of a new design, material, or physical scenario is as instant as a search query. That’s the future Godela is building.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=90799&key=user_uploads/1725296/b7ae00e6-d8ea-4354-a110-233a65568e0f)

**Introducing Godela: Your AI Copilot for Engineering.** Godela is an AI-native platform that transforms your engineering questions into computable, physics-informed models—instantly.

* **Ask Anything:** Pose complex questions in natural language or with your existing data.
* **Upload Your World:** Integrate CAD files, experimental results, or existing simulation data.
* **Get Instant Insights:** Receive actionable, simulation-quality models you can explore, refine, and iterate on immediately.

Instead of replacing simulations, Godela becomes their intelligent, adaptive home—blending diverse data sources with AI to provide insights that were previously out of reach or would take weeks to uncover.

**Why We’re Building This:** As engineers who’ve designed everything from chips and laptops to particle accelerators, we’ve lived the frustration of slow design-simulation loops. Inspired by how AI revolutionized access to information (like ChatGPT for language), we believe it’s time for the same leap in understanding the physical world.
