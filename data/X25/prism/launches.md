## **The Problem**

Creating AI video content today means piecing together a dozen disconnected tools:

* Image generators for concept art
* Upscalers to make them usable
* Image-to-video generators to bring still images to life
* Start/end frame tools for precise scene control
* Lipsync tools to match audio
* Voiceover generators for narration
* Video editors to stitch it all together

Each tool operates separately, so you end up exporting from one and importing into another, then re-describing the same scene to try to keep things consistent. Over time, characters start to vary between clips, the visual style can drift, and the assets get spread across 10 browser tabs and your local drive.

## **Meet Prism:**

Prism is the entire video creation pipeline in one place.

Start with a project, set the style once, and build your story scene-by-scene. Generate images, upscale them, and transform them to animated clips with voiceovers and lipsync all in one platform.

No more exporting between tools or re-prompting every step. Your characters stay consistent, your settings carry over, and your full project context stays attached so every scene looks like it belongs in the same video.

## Prism - AI that watches session replays and tells developers what to fix 🔍

# **TL;DR:**

Prism is an AI that watches session replays and tells developers what to fix.

https://youtu.be/xmvlRom8BNA

# **Problem**

At Rajit’s previous company, they built a new onboarding flow, set up a dashboard to analyze it, and realized that it was broken. The problem was that this realization came four months later.

This is the core insight that led us to start Prism.

Existing product analytics solutions are inflexible. They require comprehensive instrumentation and break every time an engineer adds a new flow or modifies an existing one.

With Prism, developers get insight into what's broken on day one. No event-tracking necessary.

# **Solution**

Prism uses vision models to watch session replays and identify friction. For our customers, it's like having a support engineer sit with every one of their users.

People get value from Prism in one of two ways.

### 1. Semantic search

_Rather than watching hundreds of hours of session replays, developers can ask “what are the main pain points users are experiencing?”_
