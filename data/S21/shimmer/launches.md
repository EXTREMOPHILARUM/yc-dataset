## Indy: AI support tool for ADHD

tl;dr: _After supporting adults with ADHD across 80,000+ coaching sessions, we kept seeing the same gap: people gain clarity about what matters, but struggle to hold onto it in everyday life. Indy is a free, AI-powered ADHD support app designed to help people stay oriented to what matters and translate that clarity into daily action, without relying on productivity pressure or generic AI advice._

_—_

Hi YC family! 👋

We’re Chris & Vikram, and we’re building Shimmer. Our mission is for every individual with ADHD to reach their full potential, their way. Shimmer began after I (Chris) was diagnosed with ADHD and struggled to access affordable, high-quality behavioral support. Since then, we’ve grown into the #1 ADHD coaching platform recommended by clinicians across North America.

As large language models became viable, we saw a clear opportunity. ADHD isn’t a problem of knowledge, it’s a problem of continuity. Insight is available, but support disappears between moments. AI, for the first time, makes it possible to provide consistent, personalized scaffolding in the flow of daily life.

That opportunity is what led us to build Indy.

### **❌ The Challenge**

Across tens of thousands of ADHD coaching sessions, one pattern shows up again and again.

Breakthroughs don’t fail because people forget what they learned. They fail because daily life takes over.

People leave a session with clarity about what matters and where they want to go. Then the week happens. Context shifts. Energy fluctuates. Emotions spike. Urgency crowds out direction.

For ADHDers, this breakdown is predictable:

1. **🧠 Executive function is state-dependent**: What feels obvious in one moment is inaccessible in another. Memory, motivation, and prioritization fluctuate with energy and emotion.
2. **🕳 The future fades quickly**: When goals are not emotionally present, the present always wins. ADHD brains respond to what feels salient now.
3. **⏳ Support is episodic and constrained by human capacity**: Coaching, therapy, etc. work primarily during sessions, but disappear when real decisions are being made.

Existing tools don’t address this well. General AI gives answers. Productivity apps manage tasks. Neither maintains orientation or adapts as conditions change.

### **✨ The AI Opportunity**

AI changes what’s possible here.

For the first time, software can:

* Hold personal context over time
* Reflect patterns back to users
* Adapt support based on energy, emotion, and behavior
* Provide lightweight scaffolding between human interactions

This is especially powerful for ADHD, where the problem isn’t knowing what to do, but staying connected to why and when to do it.

### **✨ The Solution**

Indy is a daily AI-powered ADHD support system designed to help people stay oriented to what matters and translate that clarity into everyday action.

![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=96820&key=user_uploads/608519/4f19593d-f653-4231-9af2-dec8ee54418c)

Here’s how it works:

1. **Future mapping**: Users map key moments from their past, present, and future to anchor what they care about and where they’re headed.

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=96820&key=user_uploads/608519/ca3c694c-2405-4ab4-a880-4248696f0539)

2. **Daily and weekly check-ins**: Indy helps users set lightweight intentions, anticipate obstacles, and reflect without pressure.

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=96820&key=user_uploads/608519/01183d22-d2fc-4650-a66e-74ae3a1d3886)

3. **Insights over time**: Indy turns check-ins into pattern visibility. Users start to see what drains them, what helps, where momentum shows up, and what keeps derailing progress, so they can adjust earlier.

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=96820&key=user_uploads/608519/00ed985f-2290-48c7-9c15-9ae24d10e742)

4. **Problem-solving when stuck**: When friction shows up, Indy uses science-backed frameworks to help users identify what’s actually blocking progress and choose a small next step.

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=96820&key=user_uploads/608519/9faff07a-2505-4abb-9067-17aee2dbc862)

5. **Progress made visible**: Wins, effort, and insights are tracked so progress doesn’t disappear just because it wasn’t perfect.

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=96820&key=user_uploads/608519/7bbaa3f4-f6a9-4c49-9161-cdd848e8de8a)


AI gives us a chance to rethink how behavioral support works for ADHD. Indy is our first step toward making that support continuous, accessible, and humane. Check out Indy to see it in practice! And [here](https://www.shimmer.care/blog/indy-app-for-adhd-wellbeing) is the full blog post!

### **❌ The Challenge**

At Shimmer, we’ve run over 45,000 ADHD coaching sessions. Each session is packed with “aha” moments—but as soon as they’re over, life pulls you in a million directions and those insights slip away. To add to this, the week is often lost from lack of accountability and members come into sessions scrambling to remember the past week. Overall, for people with ADHD, it’s hard to _consistently_ hold onto what you learn, recognize bigger patterns, or stay on track day to day. Why?

1. 🚧 **Our working memory challenges makes it hard to remember sessions**. Even the best breakthroughs vanish without a simple way to capture and revisit them.
2. 👹 **We’re focused on the present & don’t always see the bigger picture (or future)**. Without a clear record, it’s tough to connect recurring themes or measure real progress.
3. 🍬 **We lose momentum between sessions**. A coach can’t be there 24/7—and ADHD brains need constant nudges to stay motivated.

### **✨ The Solution**

   ![uploaded image](https://www.ycombinator.com/media/?type=post&id=87083&key=user_uploads/608519/ac9d5072-a251-4a37-8c1e-ae4dc05890ae)

Hi YC family! 👋

We’re Chris & Vikram, and we’re building [**Shimmer**](http://www.shimmer.care/). Our mission is for every individual with ADHD to reach their full potential, _their way_. Shimmer ADHD Coaching was born in 2022 after I (Chris) was diagnosed with ADHD and was frustrated with the lack of affordable, quality behavioral coaching solutions. Since then, we’ve grown tremendously, building the #1 ADHD Coaching Platform recommended by HCPs, therapists, and psychiatrists across North America.

### **❌ The Challenge**

Body doubling is a popular strategy for people with ADHD (it works for neurotypical people too!) to get things done. It’s basically when you work in the presence of others, and the mere existence of them gives you positive accountability to get it done. It comes in many forms, and can even look like you doing laundry while chatting with your boyfriend while he cooks.

The challenge for people with ADHD, is that they don’t do these things consistently. Why?

1. 🚧 **There’s too many hurdles**. They need to coordinate with people, it’s inconsistent, and people are unreliable
2. 👹 **They’re ashamed**. Many body doubling apps exist already, but people with ADHD feel ashamed needing this tool just for their laundry, or to cook, or to do something else that seems mundane. Imagine showing up to a session and you can barely brush your teeth, and the other person says “I’m going to finish emailing back 10 investors on closing my round and launch our new podcast to Spotify”
3. 🍬 **There’s not enough dopamine**. Isn’t this always the challenge with ADHD?! Whatever solution is out there, it’s not built to motivate ADHD brains.

### **✨ The Solution**

## ✨ Shimmer: 1:1 ADHD Coaching | 5-10X more affordable than alternatives

Hi YC family! 👋 We’re Chris & Vikram, and we’re building [Shimmer](http://www.shimmer.care). Our mission is for every individual with ADHD to reach their full potential, _their way_.

**❌ The Problem**

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects 5% of adults (13M in US), but most are still struggling to get their symptoms in check.

* **It’s not a lack of knowledge, it’s action**: People with ADHD know to make a list, they just don’t do it. We need practice, experimentation, and accountability.
* **Good behavioral interventions price most people out:** Quality ADHD coaching costs $300-600+/mo. This prices out, well, the people who need it most.
* **The process is cumbersome & not fit for ADHD**: I’ve been there (Chris): dozens of consultation calls, not knowing what to ask, dated technology, confusing e-mail chains, scared to pull the trigger, the list goes on.

**✨ Our Solution**

Shimmer is a platform that connects expert ADHD coaches with members, facilitates weekly calls, and offers accountability and support via our iOS & Android apps.
