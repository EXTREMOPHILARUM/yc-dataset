**The financial world was designed to leave women out of the narrative.** 

Women could be rejected from bank accounts, credit cards, and business loans until 1974 - not ancient history. Despite the fact that women control over [$20T in consumer spending](https://hbr.org/2009/09/the-female-economy) globally, women continue to be systemically disadvantaged when it comes to our finances: 

\-Women’s spend is disproportionately linked to traditional checking accounts, that earn low to no interest (Visa data). 

\-Women are [18% more likely](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-07-29/women-pay-18-more-in-banking-fees-than-men-research-finds) to pay avoidable banking fees like overdraft fees and other “gotchas”, costing $218/yr on average.

\-Women lack [financial education](https://www.forbes.com/sites/elizabethharris/2018/04/30/new-studies-highlight-why-women-must-become-more-financially-savvy/?sh=661c36877062) which leads us to manage our finances suboptimally, including paying higher interest rates, carrying credit card debt, or being rejected from credit cards more often. 

The original Sequin Card concept was a [debit card that builds credit](https://www.forbes.com/sites/geristengel/2021/09/15/debit-card-startup-builds-credit-for-women/). However, we sunset that product to address gendered financial gaps when they start - with young women’s checking accounts.
