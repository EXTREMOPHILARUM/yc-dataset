# Build Plan: BlackOakTV 2026

## Overview

BlackOakTV 2026 is a FAST channel—not a paid subscription service. It's a free, ad-supported streaming destination on Roku and Amazon Fire TV built specifically for Black creators aged 22–40 who have existing audiences (10K–500K followers) but no dedicated long-form home. The platform takes a 30% revenue share on ad splits, letting creators own their content and see earnings in real time through a transparent dashboard.

The shift that makes this work now: FAST infrastructure has matured to the point where you don't need $1.65M in content spend to compete. Roku and Amazon handle distribution, ad tech, and monetization. You're building discovery and community, not a studio. The original BlackOakTV failed trying to buy subscriber attention; this version earns it by becoming the infrastructure layer creators actually want—a place where they keep 70% of ad revenue and get algorithmic discovery that scales their audience.

Go-to-market is direct: recruit 50+ creators with existing followings in the first six months, launch their content simultaneously across both platforms, and use a weekly newsletter to funnel viewers back to the channel. ## Why Now?

## Current Market Analysis

**Market Size:** The U.S. Black streaming audience remains structurally underserved. The research report documents that 75% of 46 million Black Americans wanted more targeted content at BlackOakTV's launch; no public data source as of mid-2025 indicates this demand has been satisfied. BET+, the closest incumbent, has not publicly disclosed subscriber counts since its 2019 launch, making direct comparison impossible—this data gap should be stated explicitly. The broader FAST market provides a proxy: Tubi reported 80M+ monthly active users in 2024 (Fox Corporation earnings), demonstrating that ad-supported niche content at scale is achievable.

## Competition Map:

- **BET+** ($9.99/month): The most direct competitor. Weakness: perceived as legacy corporate media by Black millennials and Gen Z; content quality inconsistent; owned by Paramount, which is in financial distress as of 2025, creating uncertainty about content investment.
- **Tubi (Black content vertical)**: Free and well-distributed. Weakness: not curated for Black audiences specifically; algorithmic, not culturally intentional; no creator community.
- **YouTube**: Massive Black creator presence. Weakness: hostile comment environments (the original insight Ometu identified) persist; algorithmic inequity documented by creators; no subscription curation layer.
- **Crunchyroll** (anime): Not a direct competitor, but the most instructive precedent—a niche FAST/SVOD hybrid that built audience on free content before converting to paid, reaching 13M paid subscribers by 2024 (Sony earnings).

**Gaps:** No current platform combines (a) FAST distribution for audience building, (b) a curated Black creator community with revenue sharing, and (c) a newsletter/community flywheel that converts engaged readers into content consumers. Ometu's own pivot to *The Culture Economy* newsletter demonstrates this audience is real and engaged at lower cost.

**Demand signals:** Tyler Perry's BET+ deal, *Abbott Elementary*'s ratings performance, and the success of Black-led content on Netflix confirm the audience converts when content quality is present.

## Recommended MVP

## Core Features:

## FAST Channel on Roku + Amazon Fire TV

Launch a branded, curated Black content channel distributed through Roku Channel Store and Amazon Fire TV Channels using their existing ad revenue share infrastructure. This generates revenue from day one without subscriber acquisition costs and builds audience before any paid tier is introduced. Unlike the original BlackOakTV, which launched paid-first and needed a deep library to justify the price, this model inverts the sequence: build audience, then monetize. The original company had no equivalent distribution wedge; this is the structural fix.

## Creator Revenue-Share Dashboard

A lightweight portal where independent Black creators submit content, track viewership, and receive ad revenue splits automatically. This replaces BlackOakTV's below-market licensing model ("no one is retiring off the deals we're making") with a transparent, performance-based revenue share—aligning creator incentives with platform growth. Creators earn more as the channel grows, creating a self-reinforcing content pipeline without upfront licensing fees.

## Newsletter-to-Screen Funnel (Community Layer)

A weekly newsletter (Substack or Beehiiv infrastructure) covering Black creator economy news, new content drops, and creator spotlights—modeled on the flywheel proven by *The Ringer* and *The Athletic*. This builds a paying audience before content depth justifies a subscription, directly inverting the cold-start problem that required BlackOakTV's $1.65M just to reach 200 episodes. Newsletter subscribers become the conversion pool for a future paid tier.

## AI-Powered Discovery (AWS Personalize)

Implement AWS Personalize (generally available, usage-based pricing) to deliver recommendation quality disproportionate to library size. A viewer who finishes one series gets an accurate next recommendation rather than hitting a dead end—extending perceived library depth without additional content spend.

## What We Will NOT Build:

- A standalone paid SVOD app (no paid subscription tier until FAST channel reaches 500K monthly active viewers)
- Proprietary mobile apps at launch (use Roku/Fire TV native infrastructure)
- Original commissioned content (licensed and creator-submitted only for 18 months)

## Success Metrics:

- 250K monthly active viewers on FAST channel within 12 months
- 15,000 newsletter subscribers within 6 months
- 50+ active creator partners submitting content within 9 months
- Ad revenue covering infrastructure costs (breakeven on operations) within 18 months

## Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target Customer Segment:

Black millennial and Gen Z content creators (ages 22–40) with existing audiences of 10K–500K followers on YouTube or TikTok who lack a dedicated long-form distribution home. This is deliberately narrower than BlackOakTV's original dual focus on viewers and creators simultaneously. Winning creators first solves the content supply problem; their existing audiences solve the initial viewer acquisition problem. The research report identifies this chicken-and-egg trap as the original company's core structural failure—starting with creators who bring their own audiences breaks it.

## Primary Distribution Channel + Tactics:

Launch the FAST channel on Roku (80M+ active accounts) and Amazon Fire TV simultaneously using their self-serve channel creation tools. Creator outreach is the primary acquisition tactic: identify 20 Black YouTube creators with 50K–500K subscribers, offer them a revenue-share deal with transparent dashboard access, and leverage their existing audiences to seed initial viewership. Ometu's *The Culture Economy* newsletter demonstrates this audience is reachable through creator-economy media; pitch coverage there and in similar outlets (Trapital, The Plug) rather than general press.

## Pricing Strategy:

FAST-first, paid-optional. Launch entirely free with ad revenue share. Introduce an optional $3.99/month ad-free tier only after reaching 250K monthly active viewers—a threshold that proves audience retention before asking for payment. This is $1/month below the original BlackOakTV price, justified by the existence of a free alternative that builds habit first. The Crunchyroll precedent (free simulcast → paid premium) validates this sequencing.

## Differentiation vs. 2026 Competitors:

Against BET+: independent creator ownership model and transparent revenue sharing vs. corporate content control. Against Tubi's Black content: intentional curation and creator community vs. algorithmic aggregation. Against YouTube: a safe, curated environment without hostile comment infrastructure—the original insight Ometu identified at Google that motivated BlackOakTV's founding, and which remains unaddressed by YouTube in 2026.
